## 2. Information we collect

### 2.1 Information you provide directly

| Data | Why we have it |
| --- | --- |
| Email address | Sign in, password recovery, account identification |
| Phone number (optional) | Sign in via SMS one-time-password |
| Display name | Shown to other members of groups you join |
| Group name and members | The groups you create or join, who's in them, their role |
| Expense records | Title, amount, currency, category, date, who paid, how it's split, who owes whom |
| Receipt photos (optional) | Uploaded image attached to an expense; visible to other members of the same group |
| Settings | Your appearance preference (light/dark/system) |

### 2.2 Information collected automatically

When you use the Service we automatically receive:

- A Firebase authentication identifier (a random string Google issues to identify your sign-in session — this is not your email or phone)
- Device-level diagnostic information sent by Firebase SDKs, such as your IP address, app version, and operating-system version (used for fraud prevention, authentication-state management, and SMS verification)
- For phone sign-in, your phone carrier's SMS delivery metadata (handled by Firebase Authentication)

### 2.3 Information from third parties

We do not buy or receive personal information about you from advertising networks, data brokers, or social networks. If you sign in using a future Google or Apple Sign-In option, we receive your email and display name from that provider as part of the sign-in handshake; we use that information only to create or unlock your account.

### 2.4 Information we do **not** collect

We do not collect your contacts, calendar, location, browsing history outside the app, or biometric data.

The Service includes a usage gate that requires users to view rewarded advertisements after a free daily allowance. When ad-supported features ship, advertisements will be served by **Google AdMob**. When AdMob is enabled, the following applies:

- AdMob will collect and process device identifiers (IDFA on iOS, Android Advertising ID on Android), coarse location derived from IP address, ad-interaction events, and similar information necessary to serve ads.

- In the European Economic Area, United Kingdom, and Switzerland, you will see a consent screen powered by Google's User Messaging Platform that lets you choose whether to receive personalized or non-personalized ads.
- AdMob's processing of personal data is governed by [Google's Privacy & Terms](https://policies.google.com/privacy) and the [How Google uses information from sites or apps that use our services](https://policies.google.com/technologies/partner-sites) notice.

If AdMob is not yet integrated at the time you read this, no advertising data is being collected and this section will apply from the date AdMob is enabled in the Service.

## 5. How your information is shared

We share your information only as described below.

### 5.1 With other members of your groups

Anyone you invite into a group, and anyone an admin of a group you join adds, can see:

- Your display name, email, and phone number (whichever you provided when signing up)
- Every expense in that group, including expenses you paid for and your share of expenses paid by others
- Receipt photos you attach to expenses in that group
- Your computed balance with each other member

This is the core function of the app — sharing this information with your group is what the Service does. If you do not want a specific group to see this information, do not join or stay in that group.

### 5.2 With service providers

We use the following third-party processors to operate the Service:

- **Google Firebase** (Authentication, Cloud Firestore, Cloud Storage) — stores your account, group data, expense records, and receipt photos in Google data centres. Firebase's processing is described at [https://firebase.google.com/support/privacy](https://firebase.google.com/support/privacy).
- **Google AdMob** — when enabled, serves rewarded advertisements and collects the information described in Section 4.
- **Apple Push Notification Service** and **Google Firebase Cloud Messaging** — if push notifications are enabled in a future version, used to deliver them.

These providers may process your data outside your country of residence, including in the United States. We rely on Google's standard contractual clauses and equivalent transfer mechanisms to protect this data.

### 5.3 For legal reasons

We may disclose information if required by law, court order, or to protect the rights, property, or safety of users, ourselves, or the public.

### 5.4 We do **not** sell your personal information

We do not sell or rent your personal information to anyone, and we do not allow third parties to use it for their own marketing.

## 6. Data retention

- **Account data** (email, phone, display name) is retained while your account exists.
- **Group memberships and expense records** are retained while the group exists.
- **Receipt photos** are retained while the corresponding expense exists.
- **Authentication logs and abuse-prevention records** are retained by Firebase for up to 30 days.

When you delete your account (see Section 7), we delete your sign-in credentials, your member records in every group you belonged to, and your private index of groups. Expense records you created remain in those groups so that remaining members can still calculate and settle balances, but they no longer link back to your personal information — they appear as paid by "Removed member."

You can also leave a single group without deleting your account; doing so removes only your member record from that group.

## 8. Security

We protect your data using industry-standard measures:

- All traffic between your device and our servers is encrypted in transit using TLS.
- Data at rest in Firebase is encrypted by Google.
- Firestore and Storage access is governed by rules that enforce membership before any read or write — see our [Firestore security rules](firestore.rules) and [Storage security rules](storage.rules) if hosted alongside this document.
- Passwords are never stored by us; Firebase Authentication handles password hashing and verification.

No system is perfectly secure. If we ever experience a breach involving your personal data, we will notify you and the relevant regulators within the timeframes required by applicable law.
